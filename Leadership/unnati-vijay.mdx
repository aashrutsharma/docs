Unnati Vijay runs the operational side of building a national infrastructure platform — which, in practice, means managing everything that happens between a product decision and the world it lands in.

That includes institutional partnerships, government alignment, regulatory navigation, pilot strategy, and the cross-functional coordination between engineering, policy, and design. She co-designed the student-facing workflow on Superadmission — the experience that determines whether a first-generation student in a Tier-3 city can navigate the platform without external help.

She is 20 years old.

---

## What she does

Unnati leads Superadmission's operations and external engagement. In practice, that spans a wide range:

**Pilot strategy and institutional relationships.** Superadmission's path to scale runs through counselling authorities — state CET cells, NEET counselling bodies, and central counselling systems. Unnati manages those relationships: what a pilot requires from the authority's side, what Superadmission commits to in return, and how outcomes are measured. She understands the political economy of getting a government institution to change how it operates — and she does not pretend it is simple.

**Government and policy alignment.** The platform is designed to operationalise NEP 2020's unified digital admissions mandate and integrate with APAAR, DigiLocker, and NDEAR. Unnati handles the alignment discussions that connect the product's design to those policy frameworks.

**Student-facing product design.** She designed the "one-click admission" workflow — the user-facing logic that reduces the admission process to its essential steps without losing any of its complexity on the backend. Her design principle: if a student who has never done this before cannot figure out what to do next without asking anyone, the workflow is not done.

**Regulatory compliance.** The platform handles sensitive data for millions of students. DPDP Act 2023 alignment, consent architecture, data minimisation — Unnati oversees the compliance layer that makes Superadmission trustworthy to the government bodies it needs to work with.

## Background

Before Superadmission, Unnati co-founded College Cult with Aashrut — the student admissions guidance service that gave both founders their understanding of what the admissions process actually costs students. She led community building and student engagement there, developing a direct understanding of how students from different backgrounds experience the admissions system.

She interned at the Muskurahat Foundation, a nonprofit supporting education and shelter programmes for underprivileged children, where she led donor outreach and fundraising. She executed campaigns that translated the organisation's work into compelling cases for support — developing the storytelling and partnership skills she now applies to government and institutional engagement.

She is a published author. Her poem on adoption and social inclusion was selected through editorial review for inclusion in APC Books' "From the Class Room" anthology — chosen to represent student voices from across India.

## On operations at this stage

Building the operations function of a pre-launch infrastructure platform means working without the clarity that comes with an established category. There is no playbook for how to get a state counselling authority to pilot a system that has never been piloted before. There is no template for how to explain NDEAR alignment to an official who has heard DPI described many ways and trusted none of them.

What Unnati brings to those conversations is specificity. She knows the platform's design at the level of detail that matters to a technical evaluator. She knows the research — 5,247 students, 218 institutions, 18 states — that grounds every claim. And she knows how to make the case not just for what the platform does, but for why it is worth the institutional risk of being first.

In her words

> "The operational challenge at this stage is not logistics. It is trust. Government institutions have seen technology platforms promise transformation and deliver complications. Our job is to show, specifically, why this is different — and then prove it in a pilot."
